Weekend Locations in Delhi

  • India Gate
  • Qutub Minar
  • Lotus Temple

India Gate

At the centre of New Delhi stands the 42 m high India Gate, an “Arc-de-Triomphe” like archway in the middle of a crossroad. Almost similar to its French counterpart, it commemorates the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army during the World War I. The memorial bears the names of more than 13,516 British and Indian soldiers killed in the Northwestern Frontier in the Afghan war of 1919.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the tallest minarets in the world. It is located in Mehrauli, Delhi, and is surrounded by several historical structures. The tower is made of red sandstone and marble, with intricate carvings and inscriptions. Visitors can climb to the top for a panoramic view of Delhi.

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple is a stunning Bahá'í House of Worship known for its unique lotus-like design. It is a place for meditation and prayer, welcoming people of all religions. The serene ambiance and beautiful architecture make it a must-visit destination in Delhi.
